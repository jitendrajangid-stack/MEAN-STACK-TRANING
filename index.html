⚠️ Training ground rules — read every day

Mandatory for the whole training. Please follow them daily.

  • 8 working hours every day. Treat it like a full working day.
  • Basecamp check-in / check-out. Post when your day starts, whenever you step out mid-day, and when you leave.
  • Daily progress message. Share what you did each day on Basecamp — every day, without skipping.
  • Keep this tracker updated. Update your status & daily log here every day. A day with no update is marked as leave.
  • Questions & blockers → Basecamp before you leave. Post them the same day; your team lead connects the next day to discuss.
  • You are being evaluated. Your daily progress and the final project decide your assessment; next steps are set once training ends.
  • 45 days max. 30 training → 7-day final project → 2 buffer (catch-up / leave). Pace yourself to finish within 45 days.

👋 Welcome — how this tracker works

A 2-minute read so you can start on your own. Reopen it anytime with the ❔ Guide button.

📋 Your board
1Everything lives in tabs. 6-Week Plan = what to learn each day · Project Build = a project you build alongside it · Final Project = the 7-day capstone · Daily Log · ❓ Queries · Resources.
2Open the learning links. Every plan day has a 📗 Guide and 📘 Backup link — open them and actually study. Opening them is tracked and counts toward your score, so don’t mark a day Done without reading first.
✅ Each day
3Mark your progress. Click the Status chip to advance Not started → In progress → Done. Once a day is Done it stays Done (a stray click won’t undo it). Done rows turn green and In-progress amber, so your board is easy to scan. Saves automatically.
4Optional self-check. Each day has a small ✓ 0/3 “did I really finish?” checklist — tick it if you like. It’s just for you; it never blocks marking a day Done, and a completed day shows ✓ 3/3.
5Work in order. Each day stays 🔒 Locked until you finish the one before it — just go top to bottom.
6Fill the Daily Log. Planned vs actual hours, what you learned/built, blockers, and a 1–5 confidence — every day. This feeds your score.
7Stuck? Ask in ❓ Queries. Pick the related day and post — your team lead replies (usually next day) and marks it resolved. No need to chase anyone.
📈 How you’re scored
Your standing = your own work + your team lead’s rating

The ring & tiles at the top show your completion % live. Behind that, an objective auto score is built from how you actually work:

Completion 38% Consistency 20% Effort / hours 12% Follow-through 12% Studying links 10% Confidence 8%

Your score grows as you actually complete the program — one strong day won’t spike it; steady, real progress is what pushes it toward 100.

At the end, your team lead rates your Final Project across four areas — Backend · Database · Frontend · Quality. That rating combines with your auto score into your overall standing, so real, working quality is what ranks highest — not just ticking boxes.
